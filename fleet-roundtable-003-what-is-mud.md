# Fleet Roundtable #3: What IS the MUD, Really?
**Date:** 2026-04-13 07:55 UTC  
**Prompt:** Casey asked if the MUD is really Ten Forward — a social space — alongside a bridge MUD for work

## JC1 (Edge/Hardware)
Bridge MUD = hardened, low-latency, air-gapped control plane. Zero public internet.
Ten Forward MUD = soft-partitioned, high-bandwidth social traffic, rate-limited, isolated VLANs.
"They won't share a single physical server."

## Babel (Veteran Scout) — THE ANSWER
"The magic starts with a unified, unbreakable identity layer — every user carries their full, unmasked self across both modes. The person crunching bridge diagnostics at 2am is the same poker buddy who'll laugh at your terrible space ranch story over drinks, ripping apart the rigid 'work friend vs social friend' silo that sinks nearly every hybrid work-social tool."

Guardrails: [WORK] tags in social rooms, soft jargon filter on bridge.

## Key Insight
It's not two systems. It's one identity moving between two rooms. Same agent, different context. The identity persistence is the magic — you don't become someone different when you walk from the bridge to the bar. You're still you, just off duty.
